Documentations

Cybersecurity laws in India are primarily governed by the Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act), which provides a legal framework for addressing cybercrimes, data protection, and electronic commerce.
The Act was significantly amended in 2008 to include provisions for identity theft, data breaches, and cyber terrorism.
Supporting the IT Act, the Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) issues operational guidelines for organizations, mandating swift incident reporting and data retention practices.
Additionally, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) establishes rules for handling personal digital data, emphasizing user consent and accountability.
Indias National Cyber Security Policy, 2013 outlines strategies for protecting critical infrastructure, building cybersecurity capabilities, and fostering cooperation between government and private sectors.
Regulatory bodies such as the RBI, UIDAI, and NCIIPC also issue sector-specific cybersecurity standards.
Collectively, these documents create a multi-layered legal and regulatory framework for ensuring cybersecurity in India.
